Reggie bush and the NCAA

Reggie Bush was Running back at USC who became the seventh USC star to win the Heisman Trophy. Reggie set the college football world on fire in 2005 as he helped to lead the Trojans to an undefeated regular season and a BCS Championship game shootout with Texas. Bush excelled from the backfield and as a punt and kick off return specialist. The average length of his 18 touchdowns in 2005 was 31.4 yards. Bush proved to be extremely popular off the field as well as His 84% first place Heisman votes gave him the highest percentage in Heisman voting history. Bush rushed 187 times gaining 1,658 yards and scoring 15 touchdowns in the regular season. Bush left USC after his junior season and was drafted by the New Orleans Saints with the number two overall selection in the first round.

Heisman Controversy

In 2010 USC was slammed with sanctions after the NCAA determined that Bush received numerous gifts from an agent during his time with the Trojans. USC got in trouble for its lack of oversight in the situation, and Bush's position coach was found to have known about Bush's involvement with agents. voluntarily forfeited his 2005 Heisman Trophy, though that came amid reports that he would be stripped of the award. Bush later said that the NCAA offered the Heisman Trophy to either Vince Young or Matt Leinart, though that has never been confirmed. The arrival of NIL guidelines in 2021 led Bush to campaign for the trophy to be returned to him. Johnny Manziel said he plans to boycott future ceremonies until former USC RB Reggie Bush has his Heisman Trophy returned. The repercussions of Reggie Bush's actions extended far beyond his personal career. The USC football program, once a powerhouse in college football, suffered a significant blow due to the sanctions. The loss of scholarships and the postseason ban limited the team's ability to recruit top talent and compete at the highest level. The vacating of wins also tarnished the legacy of the USC football program, erasing their impressive record during the years in which Bush played.
